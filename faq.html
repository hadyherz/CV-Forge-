About ATS Resumes

ATS stands for Applicant Tracking System. It's software that employers use to automatically scan, sort, and rank resumes before a human recruiter ever sees them. Studies show that 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS before reaching a human. If your resume isn't formatted correctly or doesn't contain the right keywords, it gets filtered out automatically — no matter how qualified you are.
CV Forge templates are designed specifically for ATS compatibility: clean, single-column layouts; proper HTML heading structure; no tables, text boxes, or columns that confuse parsers; and text-based PDF export (not image-based). Our resumes achieve an average ATS compatibility score of 98% when tested on popular platforms like Workday, Greenhouse, and Lever.
Key factors include: (1) Clean, single-column layout with no tables or text boxes; (2) Standard section headings like "Work Experience" and "Education" — not creative alternatives; (3) Industry-relevant keywords that match the job description; (4) Standard fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman; (5) Saving as a .pdf or .docx — never an image; (6) Contact info in the body, not in headers or footers. CV Forge handles all of these automatically.
